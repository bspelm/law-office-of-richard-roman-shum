Essential Prenup Clauses Every New York Woman Should Know

As many more couples are choosing to marry later in life, it is important that they consider what their options would be if their marriage ended. What should a woman ask for in a prenup? A prenuptial agreement (prenup) can protect the property and financial interests of both parties if the marriage ends, and can help draft and negotiate these agreements. There are a number of factors to consider before deciding whether a prenup is right for your situation, and below are some of the essential prenup clauses every New York woman should know.

Prenuptial agreements are often associated with the rich and famous, but a prenup can be useful for any couple seeking to ensure their financial interests are protected in the event of a divorce. What should a woman ask for in a prenup when considering how to protect her assets? Prenups can be drafted for both traditional and same-sex marriages, but they are particularly valuable for women with assets to protect, as it is more likely for these individuals to seek financial support in the event of a divorce.

A key feature of any prenup is a full listing of each party’s individual assets and debts. This includes personal property, real estate, bank accounts, investments, and retirement funds. What should a woman ask for in a prenup regarding her debts? The list should also include any outstanding loans or credit card debt that each party has individually. The purpose of this is to clearly identify which items are considered separate property and will not be deemed part of the marital estate in the event of a divorce, and it can also prevent disputes about commingled family expenses down the line.

It is not uncommon for significant assets such as family businesses or real estate investments to increase in value over the course of a marriage. What should a woman ask for in a prenup to ensure fair asset valuation? A prenup can stipulate that these assets will be divided based on their value at the time of marriage, rather than their current value, which could give your spouse a larger share of the overall asset pool in the event of a divorce.

Alimony, or spousal support, is another issue that can be addressed in a prenup. While it is important to be realistic about the possibility of a divorce, it is equally important for women who may have given up their careers in order to care for children or other family members to make sure they are fairly compensated for their sacrifice. What should a woman ask for in a prenup to ensure adequate spousal support? A prenup can include provisions specifying the amount and duration of alimony payments.

The process of creating a prenup is a great way for couples to get on the same page about how they want to handle their financial affairs. In addition to the basic details, a prenup can include language that prohibits either party from taking advantage of the other or engaging in any form of fraud or deception.
